# Priming

Exposure to one stimulus influences the response to a subsequent stimulus. If you read "doctor" you'll recognize "nurse" faster. This is spreading activation in action -- the first stimulus pre-activates related nodes.

Types:
- Semantic priming: related concepts (doctor/nurse)
- Repetition priming: seeing the same thing again
- Associative priming: learned associations

Priming is why the Co-Wiki's [[cognitive/spreading-activation]] works so well for retrieval. When you query "transformers," the activation spreads to "attention" and "embeddings" before you explicitly ask for them. The system is primed.

This connects to [[cognitive/memory-consolidation]] -- repeated priming strengthens the associative links, which is exactly what the [[systems/rem-agent]] decay operator models. Unused associations weaken exponentially.

Reading: the classic Meyer and Schvaneveldt (1971) lexical decision experiments. Also see [[reading-notes/thinking-fast-and-slow]] for Kahneman's treatment of associative coherence.
